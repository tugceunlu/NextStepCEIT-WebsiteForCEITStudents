CEIT380 Computer Education Teaching Methods 1

CEIT380 focuses on teaching strategies and methodologies specific to computer education. The course introduces various instructional models, effective lesson planning, classroom management, and student engagement techniques. Students will learn how to apply different teaching methods, create lesson plans, and develop interactive learning environments using both traditional and technology-supported teaching techniques.


    Course Objectives

  • Understand the science and art of teaching.
  • Develop skills in teacher planning and classroom management.
  • Learn and apply different teaching models (e.g., direct instruction, cooperative learning, PBL).
  • Assess and evaluate student learning using different methods.
  • Design lesson plans and instructional materials for computer education.
  • Prepare and present a teaching technique poster on assigned instructional strategies.

Roadmaps

Skills to Learn

üìç Beginner

  • Understand different teaching models (Direct Instruction, Concept Teaching, Cooperative Learning).
  • Learn lesson planning basics (writing objectives, structuring activities).
  • Explore student engagement techniques (brainstorming, role-playing, Think-Pair-Share).
  • Identify classroom management strategies for an effective learning environment.
  • Get familiar with teaching materials and resources used in computer education.

üìç Intermediate

  • Develop interactive teaching strategies (Jigsaw, STAD, Learning Stations).
  • Practice creating lesson plans with clear objectives and assessment criteria.
  • Learn to modify teaching techniques for different learning styles.
  • Design instructional posters and materials for teaching computer-related topics.
  • Work in groups to simulate teaching experiences in a classroom setting.
  • Facilitate meaningful classroom discussions by asking guiding questions and encouraging student participation.

üìç Advanced

  • Analyze the effectiveness of different teaching techniques based on research.
  • Learn how to give and receive peer feedback on teaching strategies.
  • Develop a teaching portfolio (lesson plans, teaching philosophy, sample activities).
  • Apply problem-based learning (PBL) and case studies in a simulated environment.
  • Manage student interactions effectively to ensure balanced participation and engagement.
